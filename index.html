Regular Case

Regular cases refer to typical scenarios that are commonly encountered. These situations are essential in understanding patterns and establishing norms. They help in problem-solving and decision-making. In legal contexts, regular cases are standard legal matters that do not involve unusual circumstances. Understanding regular cases aids in the development of expertise and efficient handling of routine situations.

Serious Case

Serious cases demand immediate attention. Examples include major trauma, uncontrolled bleeding, severe burns, stroke, or suspected poisoning. Call emergency services right away or go to the nearest hospital. Keep the person calm and comfortable while waiting for help. Timely action can make a significant difference in the outcome

Emergency Case

Emergency cases require urgent action. Common examples include heart attacks, severe injuries, choking, and difficulty breathing. Call your local emergency number or seek medical help immediately. Stay calm and provide necessary information to responders. Knowing basic first aid can also be valuable in these situations. Remember, quick response can save lives.
